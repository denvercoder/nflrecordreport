Michael Black

Michael Black was picked up by the Dalas Cowboys early in 1996 when Pete McFroyster was benched due to a fractured coccyx. He went on to help the team win the Super Bowl that year.

Michael went on to mediocre success playing for the rest of the 1996 season. He was traded to the Green Bay Packers at the beginning of the 1997 season. His start with the Packers culminating in his second Super Bowl ring. Sufferring a 3rd degree pulled-groin during practice for the 1998 season he sat out for 4 games. He retired from the NFL in 2006 after spending 9 years in the NFL and winning two rings in his first two seasons.
